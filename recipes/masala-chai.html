Masala Chai

What is masala chai?

Originating in South Asia, masala chai is a delightful milk tea spiced with ginger, cardamom, cloves, and other warm, fragrant spices.

In the States, you're likely to find it sold as a "chai tea latte" or simply "chai tea."

Ingredients

  • black tea
  • milk
  • fresh ginger
  • cinnamon
  • whole cloves
  • star anise
  • peppercorns
  • cardamom pods

Steps

  1. Lightly crush the spices.
  2. Add the spices to a small pot with 1 cup of water.
  3. Bring the spiced water to a boil, then turn off the heat and let it steep for 10 minutes.

    4. Note: Do not let the tea boil. It will become bitter if left boiling for too long.

  4. Add the milk.
  5. Bring the milk and tea to a boil again, and then sweeten with sugar, a sugar alternative, maple syrup, or honey.
  6. Strain and serve.
